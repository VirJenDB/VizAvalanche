# GC Content Trends Analysis

This repository documents exploratory analysis of sequence submissions, sequence length distribution, and GC content patterns across molecule types and time.

## 📊 Overview
We analyzed genomic sequence data to understand:
- **Submissions over time**: Peaks in data entry across decades.
- **Sequence length distribution**: Skewed toward shorter sequences, with rare long entries.
- **GC content distribution**: Concentration around ~36–37%, with variability across molecule types.
- **GC content by molecule type**: dsDNA and ssRNA show similar medians (~40–45%).
- **GC content trend over time**: Fluctuations between 40–60%, with a notable peak around 1945.

## 🔬 Scientific Insights
- GC content stability reflects biological constraints on sequence composition.
- Outliers suggest special cases or unique organisms.
- Temporal trends may align with historical sequencing projects or methodological shifts.
